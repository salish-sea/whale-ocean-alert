Laws & guidance for Southern Resident Killer Whales

Laws
  • Stay at least 300 yards on either side.
  • Stay 400 yards away in front and behind.
  • Go slow (<7 knots) within Â½ mile.
  • Disengage engines if whales appear within 300 yards.
Guidance
  • Stay at least 1000 yards away to avoid disturbing mothers with calves. (WA law in 2025.)
  • Turn off fish finders and depth sounders when it is safe to do so.
  • Refrain from fishing within 1,000 yards, or a half mile, of whales, when possible.
